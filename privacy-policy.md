    <h2>2. What Personal Data We Process and Why</h2>

    <h3 style="font-size:1rem;color:var(--navy);margin-top:1.5rem;">a) Hosting and server access logs</h3>
    <p>
      The Website is hosted on <strong>GitHub Pages</strong> (GitHub, Inc., 88 Colin P Kelly Jr
      Street, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA). When you visit any page, GitHub's servers
      automatically record standard access-log data: your IP address, browser type and version,
      operating system, referring URL, the page requested, and the date and time of the request.
    </p>
    <p>
      We do not have direct access to these logs; they are processed by GitHub under its own
      privacy obligations as detailed in the
      <a href="https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-general-privacy-statement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">GitHub Privacy Statement</a>.
      Where a transfer of IP addresses to GitHub's US infrastructure occurs, it is covered by
      GitHub's Standard Contractual Clauses.<br>
      <strong>Legal basis:</strong> legitimate interest in operating a publicly available website
      (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).
    </p>

    <h3 style="font-size:1rem;color:var(--navy);margin-top:1.5rem;">b) Email contact</h3>
    <p>
      The Website provides an email address (<a href="mailto:info@volar-robotics.com">info@volar-robotics.com</a>)
      for enquiries. If you write to us, we process the personal data contained in your message
      (at minimum your email address, and any information you choose to include) solely in order
      to respond to your enquiry and, where relevant, for any subsequent business communication.
      We do not add your address to any mailing list, and we do not share it with third parties
      except where strictly necessary to handle your request.<br>
      <strong>Legal basis:</strong> legitimate interest in responding to business enquiries
      (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR), or, where the communication relates to a potential or existing
      contract, performance of or steps prior to a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR).<br>
      <strong>Retention:</strong> email correspondence is kept for as long as the underlying
      business relationship or enquiry is active, and for a reasonable period thereafter for
      record-keeping purposes, subject to any overriding legal obligation.
    </p>
